### For Commercial and Transactional Lawyers

If you are using this Tool for commercial contract work:

- **Jurisdictional Variation**: Contract law varies significantly across jurisdictions. The CISG, UNIDROIT Principles, UCC, and national contract laws may produce different outcomes for the same factual scenario
- **Incoterms and Trade Terms**: Incoterms are revised periodically (most recently Incoterms 2020). Always verify the applicable version and its interpretation in the relevant jurisdiction
- **Arbitration Clauses**: ICC, LCIA, SIAC, and other institutional arbitration rules are updated regularly. Verify current rules before drafting or advising on arbitration clauses
- **Choice of Law**: The Tool does not assess which law governs a given contract. Choice-of-law analysis requires qualified professional judgment

### Duty of Independent Verification

**YOU MUST VERIFY EVERYTHING.** Do not cite, quote, or rely on any provision, case law, or legal interpretation from this Tool without:

1. Checking the official source (UNCITRAL at uncitral.un.org, UNIDROIT at unidroit.org, ICC at iccwbo.org, or the relevant national legislation portal)
2. Verifying the provision is currently in force and has not been amended
3. Reading the full context of cited provisions
4. Consulting case law and commentary for interpretation
5. Considering how recent legal developments may affect the matter
6. Verifying applicability under the governing law of the contract at issue

## Data Authority and Limitations

### Source Authority

This Tool aggregates data from official international contract law sources. However:

| Source Type | Authority Level | Use Case |
|------------|----------------|----------|
| **UNCITRAL texts (CISG, Model Laws)** | Authoritative | Treaty text (primary reference) |
| **UNIDROIT Principles** | Authoritative (soft law) | International commercial contract principles |
| **ICC publications (Incoterms, UCP)** | Authoritative (trade practice) | Trade terms and documentary credits |
| **National legislation** | Authoritative (per jurisdiction) | Domestic contract law (verify independently) |
| **Community/open-data sources** | Supplementary | Commentary, cross-references (verify independently) |

**CRITICAL**: This Tool covers international contract law frameworks. National implementation and interpretation vary. Always verify with the relevant national legislation and case law.

### Commercial Legal Databases

For professional legal work, **always cross-check with commercial legal databases** such as:

- LexisNexis, Thomson Reuters Westlaw, Kluwer Arbitration, CISG-online.org, Pace Law CISG Database, ICC Digital Library

These services provide editorial oversight, comprehensive annotations, and professional-grade currency guarantees that this Tool **cannot** provide.

## Currency and Staleness

### No Real-Time Updates

- Database updates are **manual** and may lag official publications by **weeks or months**
- International contract law changes through new conventions, protocol amendments, and national ratifications
- **Last-updated timestamps** in tool responses indicate data age, but should be treated as unreliable

### Staleness Warnings

The Tool includes staleness warnings when data is >30 days old. **These warnings are not exhaustive**:

- Even "fresh" data may be outdated if recent amendments were adopted
- Staleness warnings do not guarantee completeness or accuracy
- Always check official sources for the current legal position

### Amendments and Revisions

- A provision marked "in force" may have been:
  - Amended after the last database update
  - Subject to a new ratification or reservation by a state party
  - Superseded by a newer instrument (e.g., new Incoterms edition)
  - Subject to transitional rules affecting its applicability
- The Tool does **NOT** automatically track amendments in real-time

## Coverage Gaps

### What's Missing

This Tool does **NOT** include:

1. **National contract law for all jurisdictions** -- Coverage is selective; many national systems are not included
2. **Complete arbitral awards** -- ICC, LCIA, ICSID, and other institutional awards are largely confidential
3. **CISG Advisory Council Opinions** -- Limited coverage of interpretive opinions
4. **Sector-specific contract terms** -- Construction (FIDIC), shipping (charterparties), insurance, and other specialized forms
5. **Model contracts and templates** -- No standard form contracts or clause libraries
6. **Full legal commentary** -- No editorial annotations, academic commentary, or practice guides
7. **Enforcement and recognition** -- Limited coverage of cross-border judgment enforcement (New York Convention, Hague Convention)

### Impact on Professional Use

These gaps mean:

- **Incomplete Legal Research**: Tool results are inherently incomplete and may miss critical authorities
- **Context Missing**: Without commentary and arbitral practice, interpretation may be incorrect
- **Jurisdictional Blind Spots**: Cannot assess applicability under all national legal systems
- **Temporal Gaps**: Historical versions of instruments may not be available
